1 — What Is CodeCore Chapter?

CodeCore Chapter is the K–12 outreach program of CodeCore at UTEP, bringing hands-on coding education directly to elementary and middle schools across El Paso. The program is entirely free for schools and students, staffed by trained UTEP Computer Science volunteers, and supported through community sponsorships.

Each "chapter" is a school-based coding program that runs weekly sessions during the school year. Sessions are led by a trained school instructor (teacher or staff) and supported by UTEP student volunteers. The program ends each semester with a school hackathon — a celebration of everything students built, with prizes and public recognition for winners.

"Empowering El Paso elementary and middle school students through STEM, coding, and responsible AI education."
2 — Mission & Vision

🎯 Mission

Close the digital opportunity gap in El Paso by bringing hands-on coding education to local elementary and middle schools. Through UTEP student volunteers and community partnerships, we make computer science accessible, exciting, and relevant for every grades 3–8 student — regardless of background, device, or prior experience.

🌟 Vision

An El Paso where every child has the coding literacy to participate in tomorrow's economy. CodeCore Chapter aims to grow into a city-wide network of school chapters — a living bridge between UTEP's CS talent and the community it serves — producing a new generation of El Paso builders, problem-solvers, and innovators.

3 — Why This Matters

By 2030, an estimated 85 million jobs worldwide will go unfilled because too few people have the technical skills to fill them. In El Paso — a border community with immense potential — early exposure to STEM can be the difference between economic inclusion and exclusion.

Research shows that students who engage with coding before high school are significantly more likely to pursue STEM careers, persist through challenging coursework, and develop computational thinking skills that transfer across every discipline — from medicine to business to music.

5 — How a Chapter Is Started

Getting a CodeCore Chapter at a school takes four steps. From first contact to first class typically takes 4 weeks.

1
Apply — A school's principal or lead teacher fills out our short interest form. No experience or equipment required to apply.
2
Connect — Our outreach team responds within 48 hours to schedule a planning call. We match the school with the right volunteers based on grade level and schedule.
3
Train — The school's designated faculty point-of-contact attends a 6-hour onboarding session. They become the primary instructor for the chapter. UTEP volunteers complete their own Saturday training before visiting.
4
Launch — The chapter kicks off. We send 2–4 UTEP volunteers for the first two weeks, then once a month. The trained school instructor leads every session. The semester closes with a school hackathon.
6 — Session Structure

Weekly Sessions

  • 60–90 minutes per session
  • Ideally on Fridays so UTEP volunteers can attend once a month
  • Led by trained school instructor or chapter staff
  • 2–4 UTEP volunteers present for first 2 weeks, then monthly
  • Consistent weekly slot for the full semester

End-of-Semester Hackathon

  • Hosted at each school at end of semester
  • Students showcase projects they built during the year
  • Prizes awarded to celebrate student achievements
  • Winners recognized publicly by name and school
  • Families, teachers, and community invited
7 — Curriculum Tracks

The curriculum is split into two age-appropriate tracks. Both tracks are designed for complete beginners and include responsible AI education as a core component.

Grades 3–5 · Elementary

Elementary Track

  • Scratch animations & games
  • Block-based visual programming
  • CS Unplugged activities (no screen required)
  • Intro to algorithms and sequencing
  • Creative computing and storytelling
  • Age-appropriate AI & digital safety discussions
Grades 6–8 · Middle School

Middle School Track

  • Python programming fundamentals
  • Web development (HTML, CSS)
  • Intro to data science concepts
  • Machine learning at a conceptual level
  • Robotics and physical computing
  • AI ethics, bias, and responsible use
8 — Responsible AI Education

AI education is woven into every chapter — not as a separate unit, but as part of every conversation about technology and its role in society.

🔍 Understanding AI

Students learn how machine learning works at a conceptual level — from training data to model outputs — without black-box mystification.

⚖️ Ethics & Bias

Age-appropriate discussions on fairness, privacy, and why the humans behind AI systems bear responsibility for their outcomes.

🛡️ Safe Exploration

Students learn what to share and what not to share, and how to evaluate AI-generated content critically before acting on it.

9 — Volunteer & Training Model

🏫 School Faculty Onboarding

A 6-hour onboarding session for the school's designated point-of-contact before the chapter launches. Covers:

  • How to lead and structure sessions
  • How to manage logistics, attendance, and parent communication
  • How to support student learning at every level
  • What to do if a student needs additional support

📅 Saturday Volunteer Training

UTEP student volunteers complete Saturday training sessions before visiting schools. Each session covers:

  • Lesson delivery and classroom management
  • Age-appropriate curriculum for grades 3–5 vs. 6–8
  • Responsible AI and digital safety for students
  • Peer feedback and continuous improvement
UTEP volunteers visit schools — not the other way around. Volunteers go to each partner school for the first two weeks of a chapter launch, then once a month. The school instructor leads every session.
