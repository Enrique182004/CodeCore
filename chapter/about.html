Mission & Vision

🎯

Our Mission

CodeCore Chapter exists to close the digital opportunity gap in El Paso by bringing hands-on coding education to local elementary and middle schools. Through UTEP student volunteers and community partnerships, we deliver weekly sessions that make computer science accessible, exciting, and relevant for every grades 3–8 student — regardless of background, device, or prior experience.

🌟

Our Vision

We envision an El Paso where every child has the coding literacy to participate in tomorrow's economy. CodeCore Chapter aims to grow into a city-wide network of school chapters — a living bridge between UTEP's computer science talent and the community it serves — producing a new generation of El Paso builders, problem-solvers, and innovators who are proud of where they come from.

Why STEM Matters

By 2030, an estimated 85 million jobs worldwide will go unfilled because too few people have the technical skills to fill them. In El Paso — a border community with immense potential — early exposure to STEM can be the difference between economic inclusion and exclusion.


Research consistently shows that students who engage with coding before high school are significantly more likely to pursue STEM careers, persist through challenging coursework, and develop the computational thinking skills that transfer across every discipline — from medicine to music to business.

Responsible AI Education

We don't just teach students to use AI — we teach them to question it, critique it, and shape it.

🔍

Understanding AI

Students learn how machine learning works at a conceptual level — from training data to model outputs — without any black-box mystification.

⚖️

Ethics & Bias

Every chapter includes age-appropriate discussions on fairness, privacy, and why the humans behind AI systems bear responsibility for their outcomes.

🛡️

Safe Exploration

Students learn to use AI tools safely — what to share, what not to share, and how to evaluate AI-generated content critically before acting on it.
