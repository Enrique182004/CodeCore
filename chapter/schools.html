How It Works

Getting a chapter at your school takes just four steps — and we guide you through every one.

01
📋

Apply

Fill out our short school interest form. Tell us your grade levels, your goals, and what makes your school unique. No experience required.

→
02
🤝

Connect

Our outreach team reaches out within 48 hours to schedule a planning call with your principal or lead teacher. We'll match you with the right volunteers.

→
03
🎓

Train

Your designated faculty point-of-contact attends a 6-hour onboarding session. Your school chapter staff or teacher becomes the primary session instructor, supported by UTEP volunteers.

→
04
🚀

Launch

Your chapter kicks off! We send 2–4 UTEP volunteers for the first two weeks, then once a month. Your trained school instructor leads every session. We end each semester with a hackathon and prizes.

Program Requirements

We keep the ask minimal so more schools can participate.

  • ✓
    Open to grades 3–8
    Elementary and middle schools in the El Paso area.
  • ✓
    A dedicated faculty point-of-contact
    One teacher or administrator to coordinate with our team.
  • ✓
    Classroom or computer lab access
    At least one space with seating for up to 25 students, ideally with devices.
  • ✓
    Weekly time slot (60–90 minutes)
    Ideally Fridays so our UTEP volunteers can attend once a month. We work around your schedule.
  • FREE
    Completely free for schools
    There is no cost to your school or students — ever. Our sponsors cover everything.
  • ✓
    Parent/guardian awareness
    Schools share a simple one-page information sheet with families at the start of the program.
  • ✓
    End-of-semester hackathon
    Each school hosts a hackathon with prizes to celebrate student achievements. Winners are published by name and school.
  • ✓
    Feedback participation
    One short feedback form per semester from the faculty contact helps us improve continuously.

Faculty Training & Saturday Workshops

We invest in the people who make chapters run — not just the students.

🏫

School Faculty Onboarding

Your designated school point-of-contact attends a 6-hour onboarding session before the chapter launches. We cover:

  • How chapter sessions are structured
  • How to lead sessions as the primary chapter instructor
  • How to handle logistics, attendance, and parent communication
  • What to do if a student needs additional support
📅

Saturday Volunteer Training

UTEP student volunteers attend Saturday training sessions before visiting schools. Volunteers go directly to your school — for the first two weeks and then once a month. Each session covers:

  • Lesson delivery and classroom management techniques
  • Age-appropriate curriculum for elementary vs. middle school
  • Responsible AI and digital safety discussions for students
  • Peer feedback and continuous improvement workshops

Ideally, one device per student (laptop, Chromebook, or desktop). If your school doesn't have enough devices, let us know — we may be able to provide Chromebooks through our sponsor program. For some activities, no devices are needed at all (CS Unplugged activities work without screens).

We send 2–4 UTEP student volunteers for the first two weeks, depending on class size. After that, volunteers visit once a month. The instructor for every session is your trained school chapter staff or teacher — volunteers are there to support and enrich, not to run the class.

Every chapter has a backup volunteer pool. If a primary volunteer can't attend, we notify your school at least 24 hours in advance and either send a replacement or provide a self-guided activity that your faculty contact can facilitate without technical expertise.
